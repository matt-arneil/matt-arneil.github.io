Working with State of Play on *Spycraft* was a great opportunity to contribute to a project with a strong creative identity and a clear sense of atmosphere. My role involved helping turn ideas into reliable in game systems, supporting iteration, and making sure features could be developed quickly without sacrificing stability.

One of the most rewarding parts of the experience was working in such a close knit team, with communication being direct and the project moving quickly. I worked closely with that creative direction to help gameplay ideas take shape in a practical and maintainable way, while also making sure the technical side stayed flexible enough to support changes as development progressed.

Developing *Spycraft* also meant balancing the needs of moment to moment gameplay with the wider structure of the project. That included thinking carefully about how systems connected together, how content could be supported efficiently, and how to keep the game robust as new features were added. I enjoy this side of development a lot, especially in a small team where good technical decisions have a direct impact on how quickly the whole project can move forward.

I supported a broad range of development work on the project, including:
- Gameplay systems
- Core project structure
- Designer support and implementation workflows
- Feature integration
- UI and menus
- Scene and content setup
- General debugging, iteration, and polish
