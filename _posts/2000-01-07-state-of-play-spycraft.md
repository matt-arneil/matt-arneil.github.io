Working with State of Play on *Spycraft* was a great opportunity to contribute to a project with a strong creative identity and a clear sense of atmosphere. The team was very small, so everyone had to take on a lot of responsibility across the whole project. My role involved helping turn ideas into reliable in game systems, supporting iteration, and making sure features could be developed quickly without sacrificing stability.

A very rewarding part of the experience was working in such a close knit team, with communication being direct and the project moving quickly. I worked closely with that creative direction to help gameplay ideas take shape in a practical and maintainable way, while also making sure the technical side stayed flexible enough to support changes as development progressed.

One of the most interesting technical aspects of the project was that *Spycraft* was being built for the new *board* platform. That meant developing for hardware and software that were still pre-release and had not yet been fully finalised, which brought a lot of uncertainty and required a flexible approach to problem solving. It was exciting to work on something so new, but it also meant we had to adapt quickly as the platform evolved during development.

What made *board* especially novel was the way it combined a screen based game with physical pieces placed and moved by the player. Supporting that kind of hybrid interaction created design and technical challenges that felt very different from a more conventional digital game. A lot of the work was about making that relationship between the physical and digital sides feel intuitive, reliable, and satisfying to use.

Developing *Spycraft* also meant balancing the needs of moment to moment gameplay with the wider structure of the project. That included thinking carefully about how systems connected together, how content could be supported efficiently, and how to keep the game robust as new features were added. I enjoy this side of development a lot, especially in a small team where good technical decisions have a direct impact on how quickly the whole project can move forward.

I supported a broad range of development work on the project, including:
- Gameplay systems
- Core project structure
- Designer support and implementation workflows
- Feature integration
- Board platform development
- Physical and digital interaction support
- UI and menus
- Scene and content setup
- General debugging, iteration, and polish
